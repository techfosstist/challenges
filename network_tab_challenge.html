Protocol: The Transient Echo

Modern applications are inherently chatty. Every interaction you perform triggers a cascade of background requests to remote services. While the visual layer of the web is designed for humans, the circuitry of the stream is where the machines communicate in raw, unfiltered data.

A common mistake is assuming that what is hidden from the visual interface is truly gone. An operative knows that every action sends ripples through the digital ether. By feeling the pulse of the exchange and capturing the shadows of the silent dialogue, one can catch fragments of data that were never meant to be rendered on screen.
